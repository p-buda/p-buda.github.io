Selecting the color scheme for a website

Find Your Color Scheme

Choosing the colors for your website can be fun. There are millions of colors to choose from after all.

Follow these steps to design a color scheme for a website.

  1. Choose one base color for the website (either red, green or blue).
  2. Go to the Dribble colors web page.
  3. Click on the icon of the water drop in the top-middle section of the page to see the palette with color shades.
  4. Click the shade of your chosen base color that you like the best.
  5. Click Update button to refresh the page. You should now see many examples of designs featuring your chosen shade and similar for your inspiration.
  6. Copy the 6-digit value that appears by the water drop icon. It is the hex value of your chosen shade.
  7. Now go to Colors on the Web's Color Wizard page.
  8. Paste your value in the textbox and click Calculate button.
  9. Look at the color schemes resulting from your chosen shade.
  10. To keep things simple, focus on the Complementary color scheme. The first hex value is your chosen shade from the previous steps. It could be used for large areas of your website, like backgrounds of different sections or the entire page.
  11. Choose one of the two remaining colors as one to be used for accents on your page, for example, buttons and links.
  12. Now let's find a dark shade of gray to be used for the text of your site and light shade for backgrounds. If you have Adobe Photoshop, you can use the method described in the article A Simple Web Developer’s Guide To Color listed in the Sources section below. If you don't have Photoshop, you can pick two grey colors from this list at W3Schools: Shades of Grey.
  13. Save the four hex values of your website colors: the main shade, the accent, the dark grey, and light grey. You will use them in the graded project.
  14. In master.css, add a comment at the top using CSS comment symbols: /* */. The comment should list your four chosen colors in their hex values: main shade, accent, dark grey, and light grey. It makes sense to put them right at the top of your stylesheet, so you can easily copy and paste the hex codes where needed in your CSS rules.

Note on a color picking tool:

If you would instead like to choose your major color by copying a color you found on the web that you really like, you can do so with a color picking tool. To capture a hex value of any color you point to on a web page, Mozilla Firefox has a tool called Eyedropper just for that. To use it, in Firefox click on Tools (a 3-bar square icon in the upper right), --> Developer --> Eyedropper. Point at the color you like and click. That color is now copied to your clipboard as a hex value. To retrieve that value, paste it in your text editor. You could also use this or a similar tool to find the base color from a picture. View Selecting Your Own Color Scheme by Rachel Shillcock to find out more.
